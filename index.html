WHAT I LEARNT AS AN RA

Over the course of 2 terms being an RA, I have grown a lot as a person and team member. Here are a few significant areas I have seen growth in.

Career Preparedness

"As I am in the early stages of my professional career, I have gained many skills that are essential in a professional environment. Alongside the networking opportunities that come with being an RA, I have learnt how to communicate professionally, be a leader, and organize my community to bring everyone together."

Inclusivity and Open-Mindedness

"Interacting with students from all around the world, as an RA, I got the opportunity to learn about people from a unique point of view: their living environment. I love to learn about how no two people live in the same way. Residents express their cultures, habits, backgrounds in creative ways."

My strengths lie in supporting upperclassmen students

"As I enter my junior year, I wish to support upperclassmen students excel in their professional careers and deal with stresses that come alongside being juniors and seniors. Walking on my path to securing an internship, I gained skills necessary to succeed in the job market that I wish to share with others."

CHALLENGES I FACED

Alongside learning come obstacles. Through the challenges I encountered, there is a lot I learnt. Here are some fields I struggled in orignally, but now have found ways to excel in the same fields.

Time Management

Balancing responsibilites is an essential skill an RA must have. I have found ways to organize my schedule such as using a Google Calendar so that I find time for 1 on 1s, interacting with my residents, and being on duty.

Dealing with Stressful Situations

Learning how to deescalate stressful situation is something that I had to learn. My best advice would be to respond, and not react! I am now confident in my abilities to tackle any situation I encounter as well as aid other CAs in doing the same.

Setting boundaries with residents

As much as I wish to support my residents, sometime for my peace and safety, I have to put myself first. I wish to offer myself fully, but whenever I can not, I find ways to draw a boundary.
